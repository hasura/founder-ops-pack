# Personal Monthly Close

Companies close their books monthly because unreviewed accounts drift:
errors compound, subscriptions zombie on, and nobody can answer "how are
we doing?" with a number. Personal finances drift the same way and get
closed roughly never. This skill is a company-grade close, shrunk to one
hour a month.

**Scope rule, stated up front: this skill organizes and prepares. It
never advises.** It will not tell you what to invest in, how to handle
taxes, or whether to buy anything. Where a decision needs expertise,
the output of this close is the *prepared question* you take to a
professional — not the answer.

## The close, in fixed order (60-90 minutes, first weekend of the month)

1. **Gather (10 min).** Every account — checking, savings, credit cards,
   payment apps. One row each: name, closing balance, statement link.
   Fixed order every month; a skipped account is a failed close.
2. **Reconcile (20 min).** Scan every transaction over your review
   threshold (default: $25) and every recurring charge of any size.
   Flag: don't-recognize, duplicate, price-crept, and cancelled-but-
   still-billing. Anything flagged gets a next action with a date —
   "dispute by the 15th", not "look into".
3. **Zombie sweep (10 min).** List every subscription seen this month
   with its annualized cost (monthly × 12 — the honest number). For each:
   used it in the last 30 days? If no twice in a row, cancel or
   calendar a decision date. Typical first-run finding is $40-$120/month
   of zombies ($480-$1,440/year).
4. **The three numbers (10 min).** Total in, total out, difference.
   Append to a running 12-month sheet. Trend beats snapshot: three
   consecutive months of negative difference is a signal regardless of
   any single month's story.
5. **Prepared questions (5 min).** Anything the close surfaced that
   needs expertise becomes a written question with its evidence attached
   ("recurring $200 medical charge, EOB says covered — call insurer,
   reference claims #s"). This is the organize-not-advise boundary in
   practice: the close produces the folder, a professional produces the
   answer.

## Worked example

First close, real pattern: 4 accounts gathered; reconcile flags a $14.99
charge appearing on TWO cards (same service, old card never unlinked)
and a gym membership that price-crept $10 without notice. Zombie sweep
finds $67/month of unused subscriptions ($804/year annualized). The
three numbers show out > in by $210 — not a crisis, but the 12-month
sheet now exists to say whether it's a blip or a slope. Total findings:
~$1,000/year for one hour of checklist. Months 2+ are faster (30-40 min)
and find less — which is the point.

## Validation

- Every account appears in the gather table every month — count matches
  last month's or the delta is explained (opened/closed).
- Zero flags without a dated next action.
- The 12-month sheet gains exactly one row per month.
- The advise-boundary check: re-read your notes; any sentence that
  recommends a financial product or strategy is out of scope — convert
  it to a prepared question for a professional.

The $25 review threshold, $25/60-90min budget, and first-weekend timing
are defaults — change them, but write them at the top of your close doc
so the checklist stays fixed-order and repeatable.
