# Tax Doc Organizer

Tax stress is mostly document stress: not the forms themselves but the
not-knowing whether you have them all. The fix is a checklist by document
type, a capture habit, and one folder structure — so that filing season
starts with "everything's already here" instead of archaeology.

**Scope rule, stated up front: this skill organizes and prepares. It
never advises.** It will not interpret tax law, recommend deductions, or
suggest filing strategies. Every judgment call it surfaces becomes a
written question for your tax professional or software — with the
relevant documents already attached.

## The checklist, by document type

Build once, reuse yearly. Mark each: expected / received / not-applicable.

- **Income:** W-2 per employer; 1099-NEC/MISC per client (expect one per
  client who paid you ≥$600); 1099-INT/DIV per bank and brokerage;
  1099-B for sales; 1099-G (unemployment, state refunds); K-1s (these
  arrive LATE — often March; note expected count from any partnerships).
- **Adjustments & deductions:** 1098 (mortgage), 1098-E (student loan),
  1098-T (tuition), property tax bills, charitable receipts (any single
  gift ≥$250 needs the org's written acknowledgment), medical totals if
  large, childcare provider statements (need their tax ID).
- **Accounts:** 5498/1099-R (retirement), 1099-SA + 5498-SA (HSA).
- **Life events this year:** moved states, married/divorced, kid born,
  home bought/sold, side income started — each adds rows; list the event
  and let your preparer name the forms.

The count rule: **January's job is writing down how many of each you
expect.** "Received 6 of 7 expected 1099s" is a status; a pile of PDFs
is not.

## Capture habit + folder structure

- One folder per tax year, four subfolders: `income/`, `deductions/`,
  `accounts/`, `questions/`. Flat beyond that.
- File within 48 hours of arrival, naming: `YYYY_type_source.pdf`
  (`2026_1099-NEC_acme.pdf`). Photograph paper documents same-day.
- Receipts you *might* need go in as they occur, not in April — a
  `deductions/maybe/` subfolder is fine; deciding what counts is your
  preparer's job, having it findable is yours.
- The `questions/` folder is the organize-not-advise boundary: anything
  ambiguous ("client paid me via three platforms — which 1099s should
  exist?") becomes a one-line question file with the evidence attached.

## Worked example

Freelancer, 5 clients, one brokerage, one K-1. January checklist: 5×
1099-NEC expected, 1099-INT, 1099-DIV, 1099-B, one K-1 (flagged
"arrives ~March 15"). By Feb 10: 4 of 5 NECs received — the checklist
makes the missing one visible, one email to the client fixes it before
it becomes an April emergency. K-1 arrives Mar 18, matching its flag —
no panic, it was expected to be late. Preparer handoff: one folder, 11
labeled files, 2 written questions. Preparer's actual quote: "you just
saved an hour of my time" — at preparer rates, the folder paid for the
year's filing habit by itself.

## Validation

- The checklist has an expected count for every row by Jan 31.
- Zero unfiled documents older than 48 hours (spot-check monthly).
- Every file matches the naming scheme — a folder that needs opening
  files to identify them fails.
- The advise-boundary check: the `questions/` folder contains questions,
  not conclusions. Any note that interprets tax rules gets rewritten as
  a question for the professional.

The 48-hour rule, $600/$250 thresholds (US defaults — verify current
values), and folder names are swappable; the expected-count discipline
is the skill.
