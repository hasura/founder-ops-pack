# 1:1 Operating System

The 1:1 is the report's meeting, run on one shared document that
remembers what both of you agreed. Status lives in status updates; this
meeting is for what is hard to write down.

## The shared document

One running doc per person, appended weekly, never reset:

| Section | Rule |
|---|---|
| Their agenda | First, always. Added before the meeting |
| Your agenda | Second. If it is a status question, cut it and read the status update instead |
| Action items | Owner and date each. Reconciled at open, not appended |
| Decisions | One line each, dated. This section settles "wait, what did we agree" |
| Parking lot | Career, comp, growth topics that need scheduled depth, each with a date it gets its own session |

## The reconcile-at-open rule

First 3 minutes: walk last week's action items against reality, not
memory. Many resolved out-of-band in threads and hallway decisions;
close them with a source note. What did not resolve gets a new date or
gets dismissed with a reason. An action list that only grows becomes a
guilt list, and guilt lists stop being read.

## If/then rules

- If their agenda is empty 2 weeks running → that is the agenda. Ask
  what is making the meeting not worth preparing for.
- If an action item survives 3 reconciles → it is misassigned or fake;
  reassign it or dismiss it out loud, not silently.
- If a topic recurs 3 weeks without resolution → it graduates to the
  parking lot with a scheduled deep-dive date. Recurring topics are
  drowning in the weekly slot.
- If the meeting becomes status 2 weeks running → shrink it to 15
  minutes and move status to writing until the agenda recovers.

## Worked example (real math)

Week 12 with a senior engineer. Reconcile-at-open: 5 open items. Two
resolved out-of-band (the migration doc shipped Tuesday; the vendor call
happened, noted from the thread), closed with source notes. One survives
its third reconcile: "draft the on-call proposal", stalled 3 weeks.
Dismissed out loud, and dismissing it means updating ALL affected fields
together: the item is struck with a reason ("blocked on the platform
decision"), a successor item lands on the DECISIONS owner ("make the
platform call by Aug 2"), and the parking lot gains the on-call topic
pinned to the Aug 5 session. Their agenda: a conflict with a peer lead,
which takes 20 of the 30 minutes. Decisions section gains one dated
line. The doc now answers what 12 weeks of memory cannot.

## Escalation

Parking-lot topics that miss their scheduled date twice escalate to a
dedicated calendar hold booked in the meeting itself. Comp and role
conversations always get their own session; folding them into a weekly
slot signals they matter as much as the weekly slot.

## Validation (definition of done)

- [ ] One running doc, their agenda captured before the meeting
- [ ] Action items reconciled at open with source notes, not appended
- [ ] No item alive past 3 reconciles without reassignment or spoken dismissal
- [ ] Decisions section gained its dated lines
- [ ] Parking-lot topics carry scheduled dates
- [ ] Status content pushed to writing, not spoken

## Adapting this to your company

Swap: cadence, the 3-reconcile bar, section names. Keep: their agenda
first, reconcile-at-open with sources, dated decisions, and the parking
lot with dates.
