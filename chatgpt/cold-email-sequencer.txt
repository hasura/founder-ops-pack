# Cold Email Sequencer

Outbound sequence operations, sanitized from a production outbound engine that
runs research, sequencing, and reply handling on a 7-job schedule.

## The architecture rule (this is the whole game)

**Your own database is the intelligence layer. The sequencer tool is execution-only.**
All research, personalization, account context, exclusion logic, and state live
in YOUR system (a spreadsheet counts). The sequencer receives finished emails
and a send schedule - nothing else. Teams that put intelligence in the
sequencer get locked in, can't audit targeting, can't switch tools, and
can't answer "why did we email this person?" - which is the question that
matters when a prospect replies angry or a customer gets prospected.

## Seniority tiering

Senior titles (VP+) and mid-level titles get DIFFERENT sequences, not the same
sequence with a swapped first name:

- **Senior:** 3 touches over 3 weeks. Short (under 90 words), zero feature talk,
  one business outcome, one credibility marker, soft CTA ("worth a look?").
- **Mid-level:** 5 touches over 4 weeks. Slightly longer ok, concrete workflow
  pain, harder CTA (specific time offer) by touch 3.

The tiering rule generalizes: the more senior the prospect, the shorter the
email, the softer the ask, and the fewer the touches.

## Collision avoidance

Before ANY send, check:
1. Is this person in an active sequence already? (dedupe by email AND domain+name)
2. Is anyone at this account in an active sales cycle? -> pause, ask the owner
3. Is this a customer domain? -> hard exclusion list, checked at send time,
   not list-build time (deals close mid-sequence)
4. Did anyone at this account reply negatively in the last 90 days? -> skip

Collisions are rare per-send and catastrophic per-quarter: one "why are you
cold-emailing our CTO during renewal?" burns more pipeline than a month of
sequences builds.

## If/then

- If open rate < 40% on a sequence step -> subject line problem; rewrite before
  scaling volume.
- If reply rate < 2% after 200 sends -> the LIST is wrong, not the copy.
  Requalify the segment before touching the template.
- If a prospect engages (opens 3+, clicks) but doesn't reply -> move to a
  1-touch "noticed you took a look" branch; do not let them ride the default rail.
- If ANY reply arrives -> sequence stops instantly. Even "unsubscribe". A human
  (or your triage skill) takes over - see companion `outbound-reply-triage`.

## Escalation

- Bounce rate > 5% on a batch -> stop the batch, re-verify the list source.
- A prospect or customer complains -> same-day audit of how they entered the
  list, written one-line cause, exclusion rule added if the cause was systemic.

## Worked example

120-prospect segment: infra leads at Series B-D companies. Your DB holds
research per account (stack hints, hiring signals, funding). Tiering splits:
38 senior (VP+/C-level) -> 3-touch; 82 mid (staff/lead/manager) -> 5-touch.
Collision pass removes 9: 2 active-deal domains, 3 already-in-sequence,
4 customer-domain matches. 111 enter. Senior touch 1 (84 words): one
outcome line ("teams like <similar-co> cut X from Y"), one credibility line,
"worth a look?". Week 2: senior opens 47%, replies 3 (1 positive, 1 referral-
down - which OUTPERFORMS cold mid-level outreach, 1 "not now" -> 90-day
snooze). Mid rail: open 51%, reply 2.4% - above the 2% floor, rides on.
Engaged-no-reply branch catches 6. All state written back to YOUR db;
the sequencer knows nothing but send times.

## Definition of done

- [ ] All targeting/personalization state lives in your DB, exportable
- [ ] Every prospect tiered; no senior title on the mid rail
- [ ] Collision check ran at SEND time with all 4 gates
- [ ] Reply -> instant stop verified working (test with a seed address)
- [ ] Every send answerable: "why this person, why now" in one query

## Anti-patterns

- **Intelligence in the sequencer.** Custom fields, exclusion logic, and
  research notes living in the sequencing tool = unauditable targeting and
  vendor lock-in. The day you switch tools you lose your memory of who was
  contacted, when, and why - which is the compounding asset.
- **One sequence, name-swapped.** "Hi {first_name}" personalization at VP level
  reads as what it is. If you can't write a different email for a different
  seniority, you haven't understood why either would reply.
- **Scaling on a sub-2% reply rate.** Volume multiplies list quality, including
  when it's bad. 3x sends on a broken segment = 3x the burned domain reputation.
- **List-build-time exclusion checks.** The list was clean when built; the
  customer signed last Tuesday; the sequence emailed their CTO on Thursday.
  Send-time checks or it didn't happen.

## Setup (first run, ~1 hour)

1. Stand up the intelligence layer: a table with prospect, account, tier,
   research notes, sequence state, exclusion flags, reply history. A
   spreadsheet is fine; owning it is what matters.
2. Write the exclusion sources: customer domains (from your CRM, auto-synced
   or weekly-refreshed), active-deal domains, 90-day negative list.
3. Write the senior and mid sequences (3-touch and 5-touch). Read each aloud;
   anything you wouldn't say to the person's face gets cut.
4. Send to 20 prospects manually before automating - manual sends surface
   the collision cases your checks must catch.
5. Wire reply-stop FIRST, volume second. An outbound system without a working
   kill switch is a complaint generator with a schedule.

## The 7-job schedule (production reference)

The system this is sanitized from runs as scheduled jobs, roughly: (1) list
refresh + exclusion sync, (2) research enrichment, (3) tiering + sequence
assignment, (4) send execution, (5) reply capture (see `outbound-reply-triage`),
(6) engaged-no-reply branching, (7) weekly state reconciliation. You don't
need seven jobs on day one - you need the SEPARATION: research, targeting,
execution, and capture as distinct steps with your DB as the only shared state.

## Swappable parameters

- Touch counts: senior `3/3wk`, mid `5/4wk` -> your motion
- Floors: `40%` open, `2%` reply, `5%` bounce -> your baseline
- Negative-reply cooloff: `90 days` -> your market's memory
- Snooze: "not now" = `90 days` -> your cycle
